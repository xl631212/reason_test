Is German Hard to Learn?

January 9, 2024
4 min read
Contents
How Hard Is It to Learn German?Reasons Why German Is Not As Hard to Learn As You ThinkFAQs: Is German Hard to Learn?

‍

How hard is it to learn German? With several foreign languages to choose from, is German the right pick for you? Can you overcome the challenges this language poses? Read on to find out!

‍

There are several notable reasons that students challenge themselves to learn German. You may be interested in traveling to Germany and conversing with the locals, opening up job opportunities for yourself since Germany has a large international economy, or simply becoming bilingual and broadening your horizons. 

Whatever your reason may be, as you begin learning this language, your major concerns may be “is German hard to learn?”, “how long will it take me to learn German?” and “how can I learn German as fast as possible?” All of these concerns, and more, will be addressed in this guide!

How Hard Is It to Learn German?

Learning German can have you saying Ich verstehe nur Bahnhof (“It’s all Greek to me”) a lot in the beginning. As you learn about German’s complex grammar structures, various verb conjugations, tricky pronunciations, and confusing word order, German will be difficult to pick up. 

In fact, Rosetta Stone ranks German as a Category II language, meaning it is moderately difficult to learn because it takes around 900 hours, or 36 weeks, of consistent practice to become professionally fluent in this language. 

So “is German hard to learn? The simple answer is yes, at first. However, this is the case with every new language! As you progress and become more confident in your skills, learning will become easier and more enjoyable!

Reasons Why German Is Not As Hard to Learn As You Think

In case you’re weary about joining your first German class because of Rosetta Stone’s categorization of this language, it may be reassuring to know the following six reasons why German is not as difficult to learn as you might think!

It Has Many Cognates

Cognates are words that sound the same as English words and have the same meaning. Fortunately, there is considerable overlap in vocabulary between the two languages. Some examples of these words include “baby,” “doktor,” and “vitamin!”

With this linguistic bridge, learners can quickly expand their German vocabulary and develop a sense of familiarity with the language, boosting their confidence and overall learning experience. 

Its Grammatical Structure Is Typically Easy to Learn

There is no doubt that German grammar structure is complex. However, what makes its grammar structure easier to learn is the fact that there is a fixed word order for all phrases, unlike in English. 

This means that once learners understand the basic rules, they can construct sentences with relative ease. The subject-verb-object (SVO) order is the default in main clauses, making sentence construction feel familiar for English speakers. 

As learners read and hear more German, they will get used to its patterns and rules and build a strong foundation to navigate sentence structures with more ease. With practice, learners can internalize these patterns and use them effectively, leading to improved fluency and understanding of the language!

Its Pronunciation Is Easy to Grasp With Practice

German pronunciation may initially seem daunting due to its unique sounds and phonetic intricacies, but it actually makes the language easier to learn. German has consistent phonetic rules that learners can easily follow once they grasp the basic concepts. 

German adheres to clear and predictable patterns, which makes it easier to see a word and know how to pronounce it! German also has many similar sounds to English, which can simplify the learning process! 

You Can Easily Immerse Yourself In the Language

To become fluent in any language, you’ll need to immerse yourself in it as much as possible. Ideally, this would mean living in an environment where German is consistently spoken. 

However, since this is unrealistic for most German learners, there are other easy steps to take to immerse yourself in this language! For instance, you can watch your favorite TV shows and movies in German, listen to German music or podcasts in your free time, and pick up a few German novels to become familiar with more vocab!

There are several ways to ensure you are constantly exposed to this language so that you aren’t just practicing your German during class time or tutoring sessions!

There Are a Plethora of Tools Available to Aid You

It can be helpful to know that you aren’t alone if you find yourself struggling to learn German, and there are various resources designed to help you overcome these challenges. Some of the most popular language-learning tools include:

  • Language learning apps: Apps like Duolingo and Babbel can be an excellent starting point for you to brush up on your vocab and improve your pronunciation.
  • Online courses: Several websites provide comprehensive German courses taught by experienced instructors, allowing learners to follow structured curricula at their own pace.
  • Textbooks: You won’t become fluent in German by simply reading textbooks, but it can help you learn and memorize tricky grammar rules or verb conjugations.
  • Language tutors: If you’d prefer more personalized, one-on-one support, there are several German language tutors that can work with you to solidify your strengths and work on your weaknesses.
  • Online learning communities: Joining forums or language-learning groups on social media platforms can provide opportunities to engage in discussions with fellow German learners and native speakers. It can also remind you that you’re not alone and that others likely have the same questions that you do!

Try out a few of these resources to see which ones work best for you and fit your schedule!

Your Motivation Will Determine Your Success

Ultimately, when answering the question, “is German hard to learn?” the biggest factor to consider is your own motivation. Unless you’re willing to dedicate a significant amount of time and effort to your language studies, you cannot expect to see drastic improvement. 

Motivation serves as the driving force that fuels a learner's commitment, perseverance, and engagement throughout the learning process. Learning a new language is a long-term endeavor, and motivation provides the necessary energy and determination to stay focused and overcome challenges.

By setting goals, knowing what you want, and staying motivated, you’ll be more likely to study regularly, practice speaking in your free time, and engage with the language consistently. You will be less likely to give up and more likely to keep working on your weaknesses to achieve your goals!

FAQs: Is German Hard to Learn?

Now that we’ve provided you with a comprehensive answer to the question, “is German hard to learn?” you should feel more confident in your language learning abilities! In case you have any remaining inquiries, here are the answers to frequently asked questions about this language.

1. How Long Does It Take to Learn German?

While Rosetta Stone argues it takes around 36 weeks of consistent practice to learn German, there isn’t a set timeline to learn any language.

Some learners may achieve basic conversational skills within a few months of regular study while achieving fluency and a high level of proficiency can take several years of consistent practice and immersion in the language. The time it takes for you to learn will depend on your language learning abilities, dedication, and immersion in the language.

2. Is It Easier to Learn German or Spanish?

Based on the language categorizations, it is easier to learn Spanish. Spanish is classified as a Category I language, meaning it takes only 24-30 weeks to learn, as opposed to the estimated 36 weeks it takes to learn German. 

3. Is German or French Easier to Learn?

Like Spanish, French is a Category I language, meaning it only takes around 24-30 weeks to learn. As such, it is regarded to be easier to learn than German.

4. How Can I Learn German Faster?

To learn German faster, the first step is to practice regularly. This doesn’t mean to just practice the one or two hours a day you normally do for class or with a tutor; it means to practice throughout the week, as much as possible. 

Immerse yourself in the language as much as possible. Surround yourself with German materials like books, movies, music, and podcasts. Seek opportunities to interact with German speakers so you can improve your fluency and confidence, and do not give up! No matter how difficult it seems or the setbacks you encounter, keep going!

Consistency, dedication, and perseverance are key to learning German as quickly as possible!

Final Thoughts

While German may present some initial challenges, the language's connections to English, logical grammar structure, and ample learning resources make it a manageable language to learn. With dedication, consistent practice, and a positive mindset, mastering German is well within your reach! Viel Glück! (good luck!)
